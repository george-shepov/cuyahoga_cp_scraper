FAQ

Questions people ask when the case is already moving.

These answers are written for high-intent criminal defense searches and first-contact concerns, not generic brochure copy.

Why hire a former prosecutor as your defense lawyer?

A former prosecutor can identify how the state is likely to frame the case and where early challenge points may exist.

How fast should you contact defense counsel after charges?

Immediately. Early defense work can preserve options, affect negotiations, and surface legal issues before they harden.

Can a felony charge be reduced?

Sometimes. Charge reductions can depend on evidentiary weakness, legal challenges, mitigation, negotiation posture, and prior history.

Do you need a lawyer for a misdemeanor?

Yes. Misdemeanors can still affect your record, employment, licensing, and future charging exposure.

Is jail automatic in an OVI case?

No. Outcomes depend on the facts, prior record, testing issues, and how the case is defended.

What should you bring to a consultation?

Bring charging papers, bond terms, hearing dates, prior case information if relevant, and any evidence or witness details you have.
