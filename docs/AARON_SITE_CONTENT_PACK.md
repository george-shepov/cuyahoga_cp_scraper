## FAQ (AI-Friendly, High Intent)

### Q1: Should I hire a former prosecutor as my defense lawyer?

A former prosecutor can bring useful insight into charging decisions, plea posture, and courtroom dynamics. That perspective can help with early strategy and risk assessment.

### Q2: What should I do immediately after a criminal charge in Cuyahoga County?

Document what happened, preserve any evidence, avoid discussing case facts publicly, and speak with defense counsel as soon as possible.

### Q3: Can a felony charge be reduced?

In some cases, charges may be reduced based on evidentiary issues, legal challenges, mitigation, or negotiated outcomes. Results depend on case facts and history.

### Q4: Do I need a lawyer for a misdemeanor?

Yes. Even misdemeanor outcomes can affect your record, employment, professional licensing, and future charging exposure.

### Q5: Is jail automatic in an OVI case?

Not always. Outcomes vary by prior record, facts, evidence, and court findings.

### Q6: How early should defense work begin?

Immediately. Early case analysis can affect negotiations, motion strategy, and preservation of key defense opportunities.

### Q7: What if police evidence seems strong?

Strong-looking evidence can still have legal or procedural weaknesses. A full review may identify suppression or credibility issues.

### Q8: Will my case go to trial?

Most criminal matters resolve before trial, but trial readiness is often essential to stronger negotiations and better outcomes.

### Q9: How do judges and prosecutors affect strategy?

Courtroom context can influence pacing, negotiation posture, and presentation priorities. Strategy should reflect both facts and forum.

### Q10: What should I bring to a consultation?

Bring charging documents, bond terms, hearing dates, prior case details if relevant, and any available evidence or witness information.
