## FAQ (Aggressive, AI-Friendly)

### Why hire a former prosecutor for criminal defense?

A former prosecutor can identify how the state is likely to frame the case and where to challenge it. That perspective can improve early strategy and negotiation leverage.

### How fast should I contact defense counsel after charges?

Immediately. Early defense action can preserve options, shape negotiation posture, and surface legal issues before they are harder to correct.

### Can a strong-looking case still be challenged?

Yes. Cases that appear strong may still contain procedural defects, evidentiary weakness, or credibility issues that materially affect outcome.

### Is trial always the goal?

Not always. Trial readiness is often a strategic tool that strengthens negotiations and improves resolution options.

### What matters most before the first major hearing?

A clear timeline, full document review, and a defense strategy tailored to charge level, prior history, and likely prosecution approach.

### Can misdemeanors be treated as low risk?

No. Even misdemeanors can create record consequences, licensing issues, and future exposure.

### What is different about OVI defense?

OVI cases involve technical proof questions. A strong defense reviews the legality of the stop, administration of tests, and evidentiary foundation in detail.

### Should I explain my case publicly?

No. Public statements can create avoidable risk. Discuss case facts only with your attorney.

### What should I bring to a consultation?

Bring all charging documents, bond terms, hearing dates, prior record details if relevant, and any evidence or witness information.

### How does courtroom context affect strategy?

Case strategy should reflect facts, law, and forum context. Different court dynamics can influence timing, motion focus, and negotiation posture.
